User tutorial

This page is a concise guide for someone who has just opened DebateIt. It matches the flows implemented in the product: account access, discovering debates, joining with a stance, and participating in the debate room.

Go to DebateIt

Before you begin

DebateIt runs in the browser. Use an up-to-date Chrome, Firefox, or Safari. If your instructor or team gave you a specific URL, use that; otherwise use the deployed link on the About page once you have published the app.

Create an account or sign in

  1. Open the DebateIt site and choose Sign up if you are new, or Log in if you already registered.
  2. Complete email verification if the deployment requires it (check your inbox for a verification message).
  3. If you forget your password, use the password reset flow from the login screen and follow the email instructions.

Discover debates

  1. After signing in, start from the home experience. You will see debates you can browse or swipe through, depending on how the UI presents the feed.
  2. Debates you are already in, waiting to join, or that you explicitly skipped are filtered out of this discovery view so you see fresh options.
  3. Use categories, search, or filters if your deployment exposes them, to narrow topics you care about.

Join a debate (stance)

  1. When you find a debate you want to join, choose your position: typically Agree or Disagree. This records your stance for that room.
  2. If the debate has a participant limit and the room is full, you may be placed in a waiting state until a slot opens.
  3. If you are not interested, you can skip; skipped debates stay out of your discovery feed unless your team changes that policy.
  4. If you leave a debate later, it can show up again in discovery so you may rejoin if you change your mind.

Participate in the room

  1. Open My Debates (or the sidebar list) to see debates where you are active or waiting.
  2. Enter a debate to read the prompt, see other participants’ positions when the UI exposes them, and post threaded comments.
  3. Respect moderation messages. Automated screening may flag toxic content; some deployments also route posts through human review, so your text might appear after a short delay.

Account, groups, and notifications

  1. Visit Account or Settings to update profile details and preferences.
  2. Use Groups if your deployment enables community spaces tied to debates.
  3. Check the notification center for replies, moderation updates, or invites.

If something goes wrong

Confirm you are on the correct URL, try a hard refresh, and sign out and back in. If the app reports an error after posting, wait a moment: the first moderation model load on a fresh server can take longer. For course demos, contact your team using the emails on the Team page.
