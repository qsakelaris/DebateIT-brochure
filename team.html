DK Lee

University of Utah · DebateIt capstone — Data Science

Biography

I am a senior Data Science student at the University of Utah, expecting to graduate in Spring 2026. On DebateIt, I led key efforts in machine learning integration and backend system design, focusing on building a scalable and reliable moderation pipeline. This included developing a two-stage moderation architecture that combines local ML models with a FastAPI-based LLM service, as well as integrating it seamlessly with the Django application. I also worked on improving post categorization and routing logic, ensuring that content is processed efficiently before reaching users.

Beyond moderation, I led the platform’s recommendation system and user-interest modeling, helping personalize the home feed through both explicit preferences and implicit behavioral signals. I also supported frontend-facing features, including category suggestions, onboarding interest selection, and moderation feedback, to maintain consistency across the full stack.

Outside of DebateIt, I am involved in research at the intersection of machine learning and healthcare, working with large-scale electronic health record (EHR) data for predictive modeling. My technical interests include applied machine learning, backend infrastructure, and data-intensive systems. After graduation, I will begin a Master’s program in computer science at the University of Maryland, where I plan to focus on artificial intelligence and continue developing scalable ML systems for real-world applications.
